Project

Human-AI Interaction Experiment Protocol Design and Execution

Research Assistant @ University of Southern California • May 2023 to May 2024 • Domain: Human Factors

Significance: This experiment was conducted to verify and validate a construct that quantifies human assistance to AI-based systems, which has not been investigated in the context of space operations.
My role(s): I designed the experiment protocol and dealt with IRB submission, created the experiment layout, recruited and trained 30+ participants, conducted the experiment, and analyzed the collected data. For this project, I developed a linear regression model for setting inclusion criteria for subject selection, created training protocols, and implemented automated data collection methods using Python OpenCV library. Additionally, I utilized prompt engineering techniques to initialize a large language model (ChatGPT) to act as mission control. Finally, I utilized parametric and nonparametric statistics to show significant and non-significant relationships between the quantifiable data.
Encountered Problem(s) & Solution(s): I identified an issue with one of the data collection methods where a trust survey was missing a response item for a subset of participants. To address this, I performed a statistical analysis, demonstrating that the final trust ratings were not statistically significant from the projected ratings. This validation enabled the use of the incomplete data set.

Project

NASA STRI HOME Capstone 3 Demonstration

Research Assistant @ University of Southern California • January 2024 to June 2024 • Domain: Project Management

Significance: This project was among the first collaborative research efforts to demonstrate how deep space maintenance operations can be conducted independently of Earth.
My role(s): My administrative responsibilities included co-developing the space habitat scheduled maintenance scenario, co-developing demonstration requirements, coordinating between project owners (students, scientists, and professors), and integrating PhD and graduate-level projects to align with the scenario. My technical responsibility was to demonstrate a bi-directional assistance framework betweens humans and an AI-enable agents, with assistance quantified by the types and lengths of messages exchanged. Especially, this work highlighted the effectiveness of specific assistive actions (messages) from humans to the AI in the context of space habitat maintenance operations enhancing the AI's ability to provide better support.
Encountered Problem(s) & Solution(s): A capstone demonstration is typically regarded as a presentation of a final product designed to address real-life disturbances. However, for fundamental research projects focused on future deep space habitats, demonstrating a final product is challenging, as it is often years from completion. To address this, I incorporated audience interaction from NASA reviewers, who introduced improvised issues into the maintenance task. Using the findings from my dissertation work, I demonstrated how the human-AI team collaborates to resolve proposed challenges by assisting each other.

Project

Guidelines for Design & Test Readiness Reviews

Lead Engineer @ Liquid Propulsion Laboratory • March 2020 to September 2020 • Domain: Systems Engineering

Significance: LPL members are primarily MS and BS students from USC who join to gain industry-like experience before graduation. Establishing review standards was essential in helping LPL members understand the effort involved in professional project development.

Summary: I developed and implemented guidelines for design and test reviews, including PDR, CDR, and TRR, to enhance and streamline the lab's operations in my role as the lab lead. To establish these guidelines, I analyzed resources from NASA and other organizations and worked with space industry professionals to develop optimal solutions customized for LPL's needs.

Encountered Problem(s) & Solution(s): LPL typically has a project turnaround of 1.5 to 2 years due to the high number of MS students graduating within this timeframe. To align with LPL's fast-paced environment, I adapted industry guidelines by identifying critical requirements and best practices, incorporating only the most relevant elements into the final document.

Project

Pyro Igniter Integration for LOX-Kerosene Rocket Engine

Propulsion Engineer @ Liquid Propulsion Laboratory • August 2019 to September 2019 • Domain: Hands-on engineering assembly

Significance: This project was part of USC's Liquid Propulsion Lab's LOX-Kerosene rocket engine testing campaign, where I collaborated with two other graduate students to integrate the engine's igniter.

Summary: For this project, I was responsible for the mechanical integration of the 3D-printed igniter housing to the engine with bolts, as well as the electrical wiring of the ignition system (e-match) to the data acquisition (DAQ) box.

Encountered Problem(s) & Solution(s): During static fire testing, the engine failed to ignite due to a timing error. We had to re-integrate a fresh igniter and housing within twenty minutes because of limited liquid oxygen (LOX), which was boiling off. Despite the time pressure and stress, my two teammates and I remained calm and successfully integrated the igniter within the required timeframe for another attempt. This time, the engine ignited successfully, although the test ultimately ended in engine failure.
